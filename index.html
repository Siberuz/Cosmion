Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system, a gas giant with a diameter of about 86,881 miles (139,822 kilometers). It is known for its vibrant bands of clouds, including the iconic Great Red Spot, a massive storm that has been raging for centuries. Jupiter has a strong magnetic field and more than 75 known moons, with the four largest—Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto—known as the Galilean moons. The planet's composition primarily consists of hydrogen and helium. Earth

Earth, the third planet from the Sun, is a diverse and dynamic sphere with a diameter of 7,917 miles. From polar ice caps to tropical rainforests, its varied ecosystems support a wide range of life. The planet's rotation governs day and night, while its orbit around the Sun determines the changing seasons. As stewards of Earth, it is crucial to comprehend and safeguard its delicate balance for the preservation of biodiversity and the continuity of life, recognizing that our actions shape the future of this extraordinary planetary home. Our responsibility lies in fostering sustainability and environmental stewardship to ensure the well-being of both the planet and its inhabitants.
